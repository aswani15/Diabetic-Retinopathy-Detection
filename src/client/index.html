Detecting Blindness

Introduction

Imagine being able to detect blindness before it happened. Millions of people suffer from diabetic retinopathy, the leading cause of blindness among working aged adults. Our goal is to build a machine learning model to speed up disease detection.

More information can be found here

What is Diabetic retinopathy

Diabetic retinopathy is the most common cause of vision loss among people with diabetes and a leading cause of blindness among working-age adults. Diabetic retinopathy is caused due to damage in the blood vessels of the retina due to chronically high levels of blood sugar. It causes blood vessels in the retina to leak fluid or hemorrhage, distorting vision. Diabetic retinopathy may progress through four stages:


  1. Mild non-proliferative retinopathy
  2. Moderate non-proliferative retinopathy
  3. Severe non-proliferative retinopathy
  4. Proliferative diabetic retinopathy
